FAQs & Policies

Clear boundaries. Clean work. No ambiguity.

Do you provide legal advice or representation?

No. DraftCore provides document drafting and preparatory support only. We do not provide legal advice, legal representation, or regulated legal services. Drafts should be reviewed by a qualified solicitor before use.

What is the usual turnaround?

Standard turnaround is 48–72 hours depending on complexity and workload. If you need 24h or 12h, expedited fees apply (subject to availability). We confirm feasibility by email after scope review.

Are revisions included?

Yes. Ad-hoc documents include 1 light revision. Retainer documents include 2 light revisions. A light revision means reasonable changes consistent with the original brief, not a change of deal direction.

Can you provide samples?

Yes — samples can be provided on request (redacted as appropriate). DraftCore does not publish client identities or confidential matter details publicly.

Privacy & data handling

DraftCore is confidentiality-first. We use information you provide only to perform the requested drafting work and to communicate about scope, delivery and invoicing.

  • Access: DraftCore does not subcontract drafting work. Access to matter documents is restricted to DraftCore only.
  • Retention: We retain only minimal service records (e.g., company name, fees charged, work category and dates) for operational and accounting purposes.
  • Deletion: Matter documents and client personal data are deleted within 21 days of completion where practicable.
  • Exceptions: We may retain limited information where required for legal, regulatory, tax or accounting reasons, or to resolve a dispute about scope, delivery or payment.
  • Sharing: We do not sell data and do not share your matter documents with third parties.

If you require an NDA before sending documents, request one by email.

Refunds & cancellations

DraftCore aims to be fair and practical. Refund outcomes depend on what work has been completed and whether the issue can be remedied through revisions.

  • Before work starts: if you cancel before we begin drafting, no fee is due (or any payment received will be refunded).
  • After work starts: if drafting has started, we may charge a pro-rata amount for time spent and work completed.
  • Quality concerns: if you believe the draft does not reflect the agreed brief, raise it promptly by email. We will use the included revision(s) to bring it in line with the brief where reasonable.
  • Scope changes: if the brief changes materially after work starts, we will confirm revised scope and any fee adjustment before continuing.
  • Expedited fees: expedited/priority fees are generally non-refundable once the expedited work window has been reserved and drafting has commenced.
